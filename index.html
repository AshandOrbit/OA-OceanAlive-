The Cooperative

A community that monitors what it depends on

Ocean Alive is a Portuguese cooperative based in Setúbal, working in the Sado Estuary – a Natura 2000 site south of Lisbon that is home to bottlenose dolphins, seagrass meadows, and one of Portugal's last artisanal shellfishing communities.

The Sado's seagrass beds – Zostera marina and Zostera noltei – anchor the estuary's ecology. They stabilise sediment, sequester carbon, and nurse the shellfish stocks that the community harvests. When the meadow degrades, the fishery degrades. The women know this before the data does.
Our Guardiãs do Mar programme trains fisherwomen to monitor seagrass – 190 hectares mapped, 20+ hectares restored, four years of continuous monitoring data verified against satellite imagery.

Four women have transitioned from seasonal shellfishing into year-round paid roles as marine educators, science liaisons, and community monitors. The programme doesn't replace fishing – it creates a parallel livelihood that makes the ecosystem more legible and the community more resilient.
